Did You Know?

Did you know about Indigenous healing and birthing concepts which were traditional in many parts of the world?




  1. Balance of heating and cooling in the body
  2. Notion of toxins and cleansing these (via fasting, herbs, ritual, massage)
  3. Personalized care (no standardization)
  4. Life force/vital energy/chi/ki/prana/jee as a substantive entity working in the body/person—and the practitioner removes blocks and enhances this energy.
  5. Use of herbs internally and externally.
  6. Ritual healing or facilitating of birth.
  7. Understanding of the place of the body within the cosmos
  8. The need for protection, secrecy or a guarded space—for the practitioner, person being healed or birthing.
  9. Non-human guidance—spirit, animal, god/goddess, saint, the dead, practitioner’s mentor.
  10. After the birth, not cutting the cord until the placenta is delivered and a special ritual handling of the placenta, appreciating its role in supporting the life of the child.
  11. Provoking the gag reflex to help the birth of the placenta.
  12. Discarding at least some of the colostrum.
  13. Postpartum massage to ‘close’ the hips and maneuver the uterus into place.
  14. Special diet postpartum.
Laura Cao Romero a Mexican nurse-midwife working with indigenous Mexican midwives and Janet Chawla of MATRIKA generated this list.
